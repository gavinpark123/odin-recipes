Bulgogi Recipe

Bulgogi, which means "fire meat" in Korean, is a classic Korean dish that involves grilling marinated slices of beef. The marinade typically consists of soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and other flavorings. This dish is famous for its tender, savory, and slightly sweet flavors. Bulgogi is usually served with a side of steamed rice and assorted side dishes known as banchan. It's not only a staple in Korean households but also a popular menu item in Korean restaurants worldwide, celebrated for its delightful blend of flavors and its ability to bring people together over a shared meal.

Recipe Description: This bulgogi recipe offers a delicious exploration of traditional Korean flavors through marinated beef cooked to perfection. The process begins with pre-sliced rib-eye beef, commonly found in Asian grocery stores, labeled as "hot pot style beef," "shabu shabu beef," or "thinly sliced rib eye beef." These thin slices are ideal for the quick cooking and robust absorption of flavors typical in bulgogi. If unavailable, alternatives such as top sirloin steak, tenderloin, flank steak, or marbled skirt steak can be used, provided they are thinly sliced after partially freezing to ensure the proper texture. The marinade is a crucial component, starting with Asian pear—known for its ability to tenderize the beef while adding a subtle sweetness. If Asian pear is not available, substitutes like Bosc pear or apples can be used. The marinade also includes ingredients like soy sauce for umami, brown sugar and honey for a touch of sweetness, mirin for a slight tang, and finely minced garlic and onion to enhance the depth of flavors. Sesame oil adds a nutty aroma and richness to the dish, while freshly ground black pepper introduces a mild heat. For cooking, a neutral oil with a high smoke point, such as avocado or vegetable oil, is recommended to handle the high heat without overpowering the dish's flavors. The beef is typically grilled or pan-fried, creating a caramelized exterior while remaining tender inside. Garnishes of chopped green onions and sesame seeds are sprinkled on top to enhance the visual appeal and add texture. This bulgogi recipe not only offers a delectable taste experience but also a simple joy in its preparation and presentation. It embodies the essence of Korean culinary tradition, making it a perfect dish for gatherings or a family meal, allowing everyone to enjoy the rich flavors and tender beef in a celebratory or casual dining atmosphere.

Ingredients

  • Pre-sliced Rib Eye Beef: these are usually sliced thin at 2-3 mm thick and it's either rolled up and packaged flat at the Asian grocery store in the freezer section
  • Neutral oil (e.g. avocado oil, sunflower oil, peanut oil, vegetable oil)
  • Green Onions
  • Sesame Seeds
Marinade Ingredients
  • Asian Pear (Korean Pear)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Regular Soy Sauce
  • Brown Sugar
  • Mirin
  • Sesame oil
  • Honey
  • Black Pepper

Directions

  1. Into a blender or food processor, add marinade ingredients and blend on high until you have a smooth purée. (If you don’t have a blender, finely grate the onion and pear and mince the garlic with a knife. Then combine with other marinade ingredients in a medium bowl).
  2. In a large bowl, add thin slices of beef and pour the marinade over top. Gently mix until the marinade has thoroughly coated the meat - do not be rough or the beef can break apart. Marinate for 30 minutes at room temperature. For best flavor, marinate for 1 hour or overnight, covered, in the fridge.
  3. Heat vegetable oil or any neutral oil in a large pan on medium-high heat. Remove the marinated meat from the bowl and shake off excess marinade before frying in small batches. Tip: Do not over crowd the pan. Cook beef through, about 3 minutes - stir frying as needed. If you prefer it more browned on the edges, cook for another 1-3 minutes on medium heat.
  4. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions. Remove off heat and enjoy!
