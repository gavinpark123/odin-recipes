Kimchi Fried Rice Recipe


Kimchi fried rice, known as Kimchi-bokkeum-bap in Korean, is a popular and flavorful dish that utilizes kimchi's robust and tangy flavors, transforming leftover ingredients into a vibrant meal. The dish is made primarily with rice and kimchi, often incorporating a variety of other ingredients that can include onions, sesame oil, and sometimes meat or seafood, all stir-fried together. The fermented nature of kimchi not only adds a depth of flavor but also health benefits, such as probiotics which aid in digestion.

Recipe Description: This kimchi fried rice recipe is a splendid showcase of traditional Korean flavors melded into a comforting and easy-to-prepare meal. Starting with three bowls of steamed rice, which provide the perfect starchy base to absorb the rich flavors, the dish is brought to life with one cup of chopped, aged, and sour kimchi. This type of kimchi is crucial for imparting a deep, tangy zest that is characteristic of authentic kimchi fried rice. The rice and kimchi mixture is further enriched with kimchi juice, a spicy, red, flavorful liquid from the kimchi jar, which infuses the rice with its vibrant color and piquant flavor. A touch of water is added to slightly mellow the spiciness, making the dish more palatable. The inclusion of gochujang, a Korean red chili paste, integrates a savory and slightly sweet heat, enhancing the umami profile of the dish. The dish is cooked in a blend of toasted sesame oil and vegetable oil, which introduces a hint of nuttiness and ensures that each grain of rice is perfectly fried, adding a slight crunch. Chopped green onions provide a burst of freshness and color, contrasting beautifully with the deep red hues of the rice. The dish is topped with a tablespoon of roasted sesame seeds for an extra crunch and nutty flavor, and garnished with shredded gim, roasted seaweed, which adds a subtle marine flavor and completes the dish with its unique texture. This kimchi fried rice is not just a meal; it's a celebration of flavor and texture, providing a hearty, satisfying dish that's both traditional and versatile, perfect for any time of day. Whether as a quick lunch or a comforting dinner, it offers a delicious way to enjoy the iconic flavors of Korea in your home kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 3 bowls steamed rice (3 cups)
  • 1 cup chopped kimchi (should be aged, well-fermented and sour)
  • ¼ cup kimchi juice (spicy red juice from a jar of kimchi)
  • ¼ cup water
  • 2-3 tablespoons gochujang
  • 3 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • 1 green onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon roasted sesame seeds
  • 1 sheet of gim, roasted and shredded into seaweed strips

Directions

  1. Heat up a wide, flat pan or skillet. Add the vegetable oil.
  2. Add the kimchi and stir fry for 1 minute over medium-high heat.
  3. Add rice, kimchi juice, water, and gochujang. Stir all the ingredients together for about 7 minutes with a wooden spoon.
  4. Add sesame oil and remove from the heat.
  5. Garnish with sprinkled chopped green onion, roasted gim, and sesame seeds. Serve right away.
